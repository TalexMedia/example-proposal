§01 The Conversation

What We Heard

Our synthesis of your audit call and the situation you're navigating.

When we spoke, it became clear that Harbor Light Roasters has built something genuinely different — a single roastery in Beacon that has become the morning ritual for a loyal core of regulars, built on direct relationships with farms in Colombia and Guatemala that Maya has visited personally. The product, the craft, and the conviction are all there.

What's missing is the story. You told us that the expansion from one café to four across the Hudson Valley is the hardest thing you've ever done — and that most of your new-location customers show up knowing nothing about the roastery, the farmers, or the reason Harbor Light exists in the first place. You're opening a new location in Rhinebeck this summer with a marketing budget that feels "small for the stakes," and a founder-led social presence that's gone dark for weeks at a time while you've been running production.

You also told us that the moments that move people — the morning light hitting the cupping table, the call with a farmer in Huila, Maya's 5am routine before the roastery opens — live in your head and in unfilmed moments at the roastery. Those are exactly the moments this engagement is designed to capture.

§02 From Your Content Performance Audit

The Gaps We're Solving

These are the specific findings from your audit that this engagement is designed to close.

Critical
Audience Clarity
Content is currently built around product launches and café openings instead of the specific person you're speaking to. Without a defined audience, each piece of content is a guess — and the ones that land happen by accident.
Critical
Consistency
Social presence disappears for weeks at a time when production gets busy. Your audience has no reliable cadence to expect — and the algorithm learns you're an unreliable narrator.
Moderate
Storytelling
The craft, the farmers, the Hudson Valley expansion story — all of it lives in Maya's head and in unfilmed moments at the roastery. There's no narrative architecture capturing why Harbor Light exists or why someone should care.
Strength
Generosity
When the team does post, it's instinctively value-first — brewing technique, sourcing education, farmer profiles. The Legendeering principle of "Lead with Value" is already in your DNA. We just need to build the system around it.
